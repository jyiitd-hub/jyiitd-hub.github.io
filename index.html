Research

Three van der Waals materials, one question: what happens to charge when a crystal is squeezed down to a single layer — and what can we build with the answer?

semimetal

Graphene

Work-function modulation by swift heavy ion irradiation; substrate-assisted Fermi level shifting in CVD films. Surface potential mapped by Kelvin probe.

semiconductor

MoS2

The centre of the work. Lattice-strain engineering of work function, defect-assisted piezoelectric response in odd-layer flakes, and 1T-phase electrochemical transistors for neuromorphic computing.

insulator

hBN

The quiet third material — dielectric encapsulation and the clean interface that makes quantum-confined behaviour measurable at the monolayer limit.

Scale-up

A cost-efficient liquid-phase exfoliation route for mass production of 2D materials, co-developed with Reliance Industries Limited — feeding downstream into functional coatings and cement/ceramic composites. Exfoliation in water, not solvent.

Devices

Electrochemical RAM (ECRAM) and field-effect transistors from 2D semiconductors, aimed at neuromorphic computing and multi-modal biosensing. Synaptic operation at nanosecond write speeds.

Soft systems

At KTH: electrochemically controlled hydrogels with electrotunable permeability and uniaxial actuation, and the programmable self-assembly of 2D materials directly onto microelectronic circuits.
